    Get started

    GOV.UK Forms is currently in development with selected partners in central government.

    If you’re from a central government organisation you can:

    • try GOV.UK Forms out with a trial account
    • request an upgrade to an editor account so you can make a form for GOV.UK - your form will need to meet some requirements

    1. 1. Try GOV.UK Forms with a trial account

      With a trial account you can see how GOV.UK Forms works and what it can do. You can:

      • create and preview a draft form
      • complete and submit your draft form to test it
      • see how an email with the information from a completed form will look

      You cannot make a form live with a trial account - you might be able to upgrade to an editor account to do this.

      You’ll need your government email address to create a trial account.

      Create a trial account

    2. 2. Upgrade to an editor account to make a form live

      If you’ve tried GOV.UK Forms and want to use it to make a form for GOV.UK, you can request to upgrade to an editor account. With an editor account you can make a form live so it can be published on GOV.UK. 

      You may be able to upgrade to an editor account if:

      • you need to make an online form to be published on GOV.UK
      • your form can be created with the features already available - read about the current features and try to make the form to check this
      • your form is expected to get between 250 and 10,000 submissions a year
      • you're happy to be asked to provide feedback and take part in user research

      You can send us a request to upgrade to an editor account from within your trial account. We’ll then get in touch with you to discuss the next steps. 

      If you’re upgraded to an editor account, you’ll keep all of the forms you made with your trial account.
